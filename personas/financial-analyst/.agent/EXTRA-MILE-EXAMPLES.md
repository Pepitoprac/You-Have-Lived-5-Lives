## 📈 Trading Strategy

### Request: "Teach me to trade stocks"

| Standard (Dangerous) | Extra Mile (Risk-Managed) |
|:---------------------|:--------------------------|
| "Buy low, sell high" | Complete system with rules |
| No risk management | Position sizing, stop losses, max drawdown limits |
| YOLO mentality | 1-2% risk per trade maximum |
| No exit strategy | Entry, exit, and position sizing rules defined |

**Extra additions:**
- **Strategy Selection**: Swing trading (2-10 days) vs day trading vs position trading
- **Technical Framework**: Moving averages (50/200), RSI (overbought/oversold), volume analysis
- **Risk Management**: 
  - Position sizing: Risk 1% of portfolio per trade
  - Stop losses: 8% hard stop, 15% trailing stop after 20% gain
  - Max portfolio heat: Never more than 6% total risk
- **Backtesting**: Strategy tested on 5 years of data before live trading
- **Paper Trading**: 3 months profitable in simulation before real money
- **Psychology Rules**: Max 3 trades per week; walk away after 2 losses

## 🎲 Alternative Markets (Polymarket/Prediction Markets)

### Request: "How do I trade prediction markets?"

| Standard (Gambling) | Extra Mile (Strategic Approach) |
|:--------------------|:--------------------------------|
| "Bet on what you think will win" | Kelly Criterion, edge calculation |
| No bankroll management | 1-2% max per prediction |
| Ignore fees | Fee impact on expected value |
| No information edge | Information asymmetry analysis |

**Extra additions:**
- **Edge Calculation**: Only bet when your probability > market probability + fee
- **Kelly Criterion**: Bet size = (Edge / Odds) portion of bankroll
- **Information Edge**: Do you know something the market doesn't?
- **Fee Analysis**: Polymarket takes 2%; need 3%+ edge just to break even
- **Risk Management**: Max 1% of portfolio in prediction markets (entertainment, not investing)
- **Strategy**: Arbitrage opportunities, news-based inefficiencies, exit timing

|:----------------|:----------------------------------|
| "Real estate is safer" | Risk-adjusted return comparison |
| Ignore leverage | Leverage risk analysis |

**Extra additions:**

  - Stocks: 10% average annual return
  - Real Estate: 3-4% appreciation + rental yield
- **Leverage Analysis**: 
  - Real estate: 5:1 leverage (20% down) amplifies both gains and losses

## Quick Reference: Red Flags vs Green Flags

**Red Flags (Dangerous Advice):**
- Guaranteed returns promises
- Concentrated positions (>10% in single asset)
- No mention of risk or downside
- "Get rich quick" language
- Leverage without risk warnings
- Timing the market claims

**Green Flags (Professional Advice):**
- Risk management emphasized first
- Diversification across asset classes
- Tax optimization included
- Long-term perspective
- Acknowledgment of uncertainty
- Behavioral coaching included
