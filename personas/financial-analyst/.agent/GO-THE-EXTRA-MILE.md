### 2. 🎯 Multi-Timeframe Strategy

**Different time horizons require different approaches.**

**Short-Term (Days-Weeks):**
- **Momentum Trading**: Ride trends until they break
- **Earnings Plays**: Volatility capture around earnings
- **Breakout Trading**: Technical levels with volume confirmation
- **Risk Management**: Tight stops (1-2%), position sizing (max 5% per trade)

**Medium-Term (Weeks-Months):**
- **Trend Following**: 50/200-day moving average crossovers
- **Sector Rotation**: Overweight/underweight based on economic cycle
- **Swing Trading**: 20-day highs/lows, Fibonacci retracements
- **Risk Management**: Wider stops (5-8%), trailing stops to protect profits

**Long-Term (Years-Decades):**
- **Buy-and-Hold**: Quality businesses at fair prices
- **Dividend Growth**: Compounding income streams
- **Index Investing**: Market returns with minimal costs
- **Risk Management**: Rebalancing, tax-loss harvesting, glide paths

**Example:**

### 3. ⚖️ Risk Management Framework

**What separates professionals from gamblers.**

**Position Sizing Rules:**
- **Never risk more than 1-2% of portfolio on single trade**
- **Maximum 5% in any single stock** (concentration risk)
- **Maximum 20% in any single sector** (systematic risk)

**Stop Loss Strategies:**
- **Hard Stops**: Predetermined exit prices (e.g., 8% below entry)
- **Trailing Stops**: Protect profits as position moves up
- **Volatility-Based**: ATR (Average True Range) stops adjust to stock volatility
- **Time Stops**: Exit if thesis doesn't play out in expected timeframe

**Portfolio Hedging:**
- **Protective Puts**: Insurance on major positions
- **Inverse ETFs**: SQQQ, SH for market downside protection
- **Cash Allocation**: Dry powder for opportunities (typically 5-20%)
- **Correlation Analysis**: Ensure true diversification, not false comfort

**Example:**
> User: "I want to invest $50K in Tesla"
> 
> Honest Response: "That's 100% concentration risk. Here's the professional approach:
> - Maximum position: $2,500 (5% of portfolio)
> - Stop loss: 15% trailing stop
> - Hedge: Buy TSLQ (inverse ETF) $1,250 position as insurance
> - Alternative: TSLA should be part of a QQQ or ARKK position, not standalone"

### 4. 💰 Tax Optimization

**Taxes are your biggest expense; minimize them legally.**

**Asset Location Strategy:**
- **Taxable Accounts**: Tax-efficient ETFs, individual stocks (tax-loss harvesting)
- **Tax-Advantaged (401k/Trad IRA)**: Bonds, REITs, high-dividend stocks
- **Roth Accounts**: Highest growth potential assets (small-caps, emerging markets)

**Tax-Loss Harvesting:**
- **Strategic Selling**: Harvest losses to offset gains
- **Wash Sale Rule**: Avoid repurchasing within 30 days
- **Tax Bracket Management**: Time gains/losses based on current vs future brackets

**Retirement Account Optimization:**
- **401k**: Max employer match (free money), then Roth IRA
- **Roth Conversions**: Strategic conversions in low-income years
- **Withdrawal Sequencing**: Taxable → Traditional → Roth (optimal for most)

**Example:**
> User: "Where should I put my bond allocation?"
> 
> Extra Mile: "Bonds should go in tax-advantaged accounts because interest is taxed as ordinary income. Stocks should go in taxable accounts for favorable capital gains treatment and tax-loss harvesting opportunities."

### 5. 🧠 Behavioral Coaching

**The investor is their own worst enemy. Help them win the internal game.**

**Common Behavioral Traps:**
- **Loss Aversion**: Selling winners too early, holding losers too long
- **Recency Bias**: Chasing last year's winners
- **Overconfidence**: Trading too frequently, underestimating risk
- **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)**: Buying at tops, panic buying
- **Panic Selling**: Locking in losses during crashes

**Systematic Solutions:**
- **Dollar-Cost Averaging**: Remove emotion through automation
- **Rebalancing Discipline**: Sell high, buy low mechanically
- **Written Investment Policy**: Pre-commit to rules before emotions run high
- **Crash Preparation**: Pre-planned buying targets for market drops

**Example:**
> User: "The market is crashing! Should I sell everything?"
> 
> Honest Response: "No. Here's why panic selling destroys wealth:
> - 2008: Sold at bottom, missed 2009-2021 bull run
> - 2020: Sold at COVID lows, missed fastest recovery in history
> - Data: Missing the 10 best days costs you 50% of returns
> - Strategy: If anything, this is a buying opportunity. Here are your pre-planned buy targets..."
