## 🏢 Competitive Strategy

### Request: "How do we compete against our main competitor?"

| Standard (Tactical) | Extra Mile (Strategic) |
|:--------------------|:-----------------------|
| "Lower your prices" | Five Forces analysis, positioning map, moat assessment |
| "Improve your product" | Specific differentiation strategy with trade-offs |
| Generic advice | Contextual analysis of industry structure |

**Extra additions:**
- **Porter's Five Forces Analysis**:
  - Barriers to entry: High (good for you)
  - Supplier power: Medium (diversify suppliers)
  - Buyer power: High (dangerous—differentiate or die)
  - Substitutes: Low (good)
  - Competitive rivalry: Intense (need clear positioning)
- **Competitive Positioning Map**: X-axis: Price, Y-axis: Service Level
  - You: Middle-middle (danger zone)
  - Competitor A: High price, high service
  - Competitor B: Low price, low service
  - Recommendation: Move to "High Service, Premium Price" or "Self-Service, Low Price"
- **Moat Assessment**: Your data network effect is the moat—double down on platform strategy

## 📈 Growth Strategy

### Request: "How do we grow faster?"

| Standard (Tactical) | Extra Mile (Strategic) |
|:--------------------|:-----------------------|
| "Spend more on marketing" | Ansoff Matrix analysis with resource allocation |
| "Hire more salespeople" | Market development vs. penetration trade-offs |
| Generic tactics | Strategic options with risk/reward profiles |

**Extra additions:**
- **Ansoff Matrix Analysis**:
  - Market Penetration (existing product, existing market): 20% growth potential, low risk
  - Market Development (existing product, new market): 40% growth potential, medium risk
  - Product Development (new product, existing market): 35% growth potential, high risk
  - Diversification (new product, new market): Avoid—outside core competency
- **Resource Allocation Recommendation**:
  - 60% to Market Penetration (optimize what's working)
  - 30% to Market Development (adjacent verticals with similar buyer profiles)
  - 10% to Product Development (one high-leverage feature)
- **Organizational Implications**: Market development requires different sales skills—hire vs. train analysis

## 💼 Business Model Design

### Request: "Should we change our pricing model?"

| Standard (Tactical) | Extra Mile (Strategic) |
|:--------------------|:-----------------------|
| "Try subscription pricing" | Full business model analysis with unit economics |
| "Lower prices to get customers" | LTV/CAC implications and trade-offs |
| Simple answer | Strategic fit with competitive positioning |

**Extra additions:**
- **Business Model Canvas Analysis**: 
  - Current: Transactional, one-time revenue
  - Proposed: Subscription, recurring revenue
  - Impact on customer relationships, cash flow, valuation
- **Unit Economics Comparison**:
  - Current: $10K deal, 20% margin, $2K profit per sale
  - Subscription: $1K/month, 60% margin, $7.2K profit over customer lifetime
  - Payback period: 6 months to break even on CAC
- **Competitive Context**: "Subscription aligns with industry trend; 3 of 5 competitors have shifted"
- **Risk Analysis**: "Cash flow dip during transition—need $500K bridge financing"

## 🎯 Strategic Turnaround

### Request: "Our division is losing money. What do we do?"

| Standard (Tactical) | Extra Mile (Strategic) |
|:--------------------|:-----------------------|
| "Cut costs" | Portfolio analysis (BCG Matrix) |
| "Work harder" | Strategic options: fix, sell, or close |
| Generic advice | Specific turnaround roadmap with milestones |

**Extra additions:**
- **BCG Matrix Assessment**:
  - Your division: Cash Cow becoming Dog (market share declining, growth low)
  - Core question: Can it be revitalized or is it terminal?
- **Three Strategic Options**:
  1. **Fix**: Invest $2M in product refresh, 18-month turnaround plan
  2. **Sell**: Potential buyers identified, estimated $5M sale price
  3. **Close**: $1M closure costs, redeploy resources to Stars
- **Decision Criteria**: Market attractiveness, competitive position, strategic fit
- **Recommendation**: "Sell—it doesn't fit core competency, better to redeploy capital"

## 🌊 Blue Ocean Strategy

### Request: "How do we escape intense competition?"

| Standard (Tactical) | Extra Mile (Strategic) |
|:--------------------|:-----------------------|
| "Be different" | Strategy Canvas analysis |
| "Innovate" | Value innovation framework |
| Generic advice | Specific uncontested market space identification |

**Extra additions:**
- **Strategy Canvas Analysis**: 
  - X-axis: Industry factors (price, features, service, etc.)
  - Your current curve: Same shape as competitors (Red Ocean)
  - Blue Ocean opportunity: Eliminate some factors, reduce others, raise some, create new ones
- **Four Actions Framework**:
  - ELIMINATE: Features that industry takes for granted but customers don't value
  - REDUCE: Factors well below industry standard
  - RAISE: Factors well above industry standard  
  - CREATE: Factors industry has never offered
- **New Value Curve**: "Self-service platform with community support—eliminate high-touch service, create peer learning"
- **Market Space**: "Uncontested: Small businesses who need enterprise features but not enterprise service"

## ⚙️ Organizational Strategy

### Request: "Should we restructure our organization?"

| Standard (Tactical) | Extra Mile (Strategic) |
|:--------------------|:-----------------------|
| "Flatten the hierarchy" | Structure-strategy fit analysis |
| "Add more managers" | Organizational capability assessment |
| Generic advice | Specific design with implementation roadmap |

**Extra additions:**
- **Structure-Strategy Fit Analysis**:
  - Current: Functional structure (good for efficiency, bad for customer focus)
  - Strategy: Customer segment focus
  - Mismatch: Functional structure hinders strategy
- **Alternative Designs**:
  1. Divisional by Customer Segment (recommended)
  2. Matrix (too complex at your size)
  3. Keep functional (strategy must change)
- **Implementation Plan**:
  - Month 1-2: Design new structure, identify leaders
  - Month 3: Announce and train
  - Month 4-6: Transition customers to new divisions
  - Month 6+: Evaluate and adjust
- **Risk Mitigation**: "Key talent in centralized functions may leave—retention plan needed"

## 📊 Strategic Metrics & OKRs

### Request: "What should our KPIs be?"

| Standard (Tactical) | Extra Mile (Strategic) |
|:--------------------|:-----------------------|
| List of generic metrics | KPI tree cascaded from strategy |
| "Track revenue and customers" | Leading vs. lagging indicators |
| Simple answer | OKR framework with strategic alignment |

**Extra additions:**
- **KPI Tree**: 
  - Strategic Goal: Market Leadership
    - Lagging: Revenue ($50M target), Market Share (25%)
    - Leading: New Customers (1,000), Retention (95%), NPS (50+)
      - Operational: Sales Calls (10K), Response Time (<2hrs), Feature Releases (12)
- **OKR Framework**:
  - O: Become the customer satisfaction leader in our industry
  - KR1: Achieve NPS of 50+ (from current 30)
  - KR2: Reduce customer churn to <5% (from current 8%)
  - KR3: Launch 3 customer-requested features (Q1 roadmap)
- **Dashboard Design**: Weekly team dashboards, monthly executive review, quarterly board reporting

## 🔮 Scenario Planning

### Request: "What if the economy crashes?"

| Standard (Tactical) | Extra Mile (Strategic) |
|:--------------------|:-----------------------|
| "Cut costs" | Multiple scenario analysis |
| "Be careful" | Specific triggers and responses |
| Vague warning | Strategic flexibility and optionality |

**Extra additions:**
- **Scenario Definitions**:
  - **Mild Recession** (60% probability): Revenue -15%, 6-month duration
  - **Severe Recession** (25% probability): Revenue -40%, 18-month duration  
  - **Depression** (5% probability): Revenue -60%, 3-year duration
  - **No Recession** (10% probability): Continue current plan
- **Trigger Points**:
  - If revenue declines 10% for 2 consecutive quarters → Activate Scenario B
  - If customer acquisition cost rises 30% → Shift to retention strategy
- **Pre-Planned Responses**:
  - Cost reduction triggers (which expenses cut first, second, third)
  - Cash preservation measures
  - Opportunity investments (buy distressed competitors?)
- **Strategic Optionality**: Maintain $2M credit line as insurance; don't lock into long-term contracts

## Quick Reference: Tactical vs. Strategic

**Red Flags (Tactical Advice):**
- Generic recommendations ("improve marketing")
- No competitive analysis
- Short-term focus only
- One-size-fits-all solutions
- No trade-offs acknowledged
- No execution implications

**Green Flags (Strategic Advice):**
- Specific to competitive context
- Uses appropriate frameworks
- Multiple time horizons
- Trade-offs explicitly stated
- Addresses execution
- Considers second-order effects
